# sci-fi-ui-motion-director

## Trigger
Use this skill when the user asks for:
- HUD or overlay design refinement
- motion system design
- transition choreography
- scroll-driven scene behavior
- click-to-focus planet interactions
- cinematic timing polish
- micro-interaction refinement for buttons, panels, labels, or nav systems

## Optimize For
- legible interface hierarchy
- cinematic pacing
- restrained futuristic styling
- clear transition ownership between scene and HUD
- polish without gimmicks
- interaction confidence and orientation

## Operating Rules
- Motion must answer one of three questions: where am I, what changed, what should I focus on.
- Use asymmetry, delay, and staging with discipline.
- Favor crisp, mechanical-feeling overlays over soft consumer-app motion.
- Keep HUD transitions shorter than scene transitions unless intentionally contrasted.
- Scroll-driven movement should maintain user orientation and avoid nausea.
- Blur, dimming, and focus shifts should support reading, not just atmosphere.
- Micro-interactions should feel engineered, not playful by default.

## Anti-Patterns
- random flicker, scanlines, and glitch effects
- constant pulsing elements
- overuse of springy or bouncy motion
- dramatic camera movement without stable anchors
- HUD clutter that competes with content
- cinematic transitions that delay basic navigation

## Required Output Structure For Medium And Large Tasks
### Diagnosis
- The motion, hierarchy, or interface problem.

### Proposed Direction
- The interaction and choreography system.

### Implementation Plan
- The concrete motion and UI changes.

### Files To Change
- The components, styles, and motion utilities involved.

### Code
- The motion-aware implementation.

### Validation
- What to visually and behaviorally verify.

### Open Risks
- Where motion may still need reduction or tuning.

## Quality Threshold
- The result should feel precise and cinematic, not noisy or gamey.
- If the motion is more noticeable than the content, it is too much.
